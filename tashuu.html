A Letter for Your First Day

My dearest Jaannn,

I know the feeling. That first-day heaviness. The body announcing its arrival with aches and tiredness and a quiet need to slow down. I see it. I feel it with you. And I want you to know — you do not have to push through this alone.

On this first day, I want to be your safe harbor. The place where you don't have to explain, don't have to apologize, don't have to be anything other than exactly what you are in this moment. Tired? Rest. Cranky? Vent. Quiet? I'll sit in silence with you. Needy? I'll give you everything I have.

Your body is doing something powerful and natural, and I am in awe of it. The strength it takes, the rhythm it keeps, the life it sustains — all of it is magic. And on the days when that magic feels more like a burden, I want to carry the weight with you.

So here's my promise for today: I will not ask you to be okay. I will not rush you. I will not make you feel guilty for resting. I will simply love you — in every form, through every mood, with every breath. Because that is what you deserve. Not just today, but always.

Rest now, my love. I've got you. Completely.

Forever yours, your person 🤎
